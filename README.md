This study presents an approach to the recognition
of speech signal using frequency spectral information with Mel
frequency for the improvement of speech, and proposes a novel
dialect-aware ASR system that enhances speech recognition while
simultaneously classifying Telugu dialects. MFCC coefficients
capture spectral features of the voice, and have been used
for emotion recognition, Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients
(MFCCs) represent the unique speech information in the fre
quency domain.
All audio samples are provided at a sampling rate of 16
kHz, and all audio recordings were provided in FLAC format
and subsequently converted to NumPy arrays with a consistent
sampling rate of 16 kHz. Wav2Vec2 is a self-supervised model
initially used for human speech recognition tasks, wav2vec (2019)
showed that raw audio can be pretrained using a contrastive
objective to learn contextual speech representations. Wav2vec 2.0
enables us to build better speech recognition systems for many
more languages and domains with much less annotated data, and
wav2vec (2019) showed that raw audio can be pretrained using a
contrastive objective to learn contextual speech representations.
In contrast, pooling operations reduce variable-length sequences
to fixed dimensions through mathematical operations that ag
gregate information, and feature embedding is the process of
transforming data into continuous vector representations.
As ML algorithms, Support Vector Machine (SVM), Naive
Bayes (NB), Random Forest (RF), Decision Tree (DT), K
Nearest Neighbor (KNN), Logistic Regression (LR) were used
for classification tasks. Data augmentation techniques, such as
waveform, noise adding, pitch shifting, and time stretching, are
applied to improve the generalization capability of the model,
and data augmentation offers a promising approach to mitigate
class imbalance by artificially increasing the size of the minority
class.
Wav2Vec 1.0, while generally outperformed by Wav2Vec
2.0, still provided a more efficient alternative with competitive
performance, and the results show that combining all three
deep embeddings achieves an AUROC of 89.49% and accuracy.
This study presents a deep learning framework for speaker
diarization, and this work explores the effectiveness of CAE with
SSL-based speech representations to obtain improved AWEs, and
Whisper large embeddings paired with a LogR classifier demon
strated an impressive average accuracy. This study highlights the
effectiveness of self-supervised learning strategies for tasks such
as automatic speech recognition, and a deep learning architecture
that merges attentional and residual convolutional networks for
age and gender classification.
